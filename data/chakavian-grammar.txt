Rule: m's at the end of words become n's
Example:
Ja sam = Ja san
osam = osan
s ženom = s ženon
vidim = vidin

Rule: đ becomes j
Example:
mlađi=>mlaji
među=>meju

Rule: The sounds ć and đ actually do not exist separately from č and dž

Rule: genitive plural in the feminine and neuter takes a zero ending: 
Example: žena = žen, sela = sel

Rule: dative/instrumental plurals are also somewhat different.

Rule: Masculine and neuter nouns can have an alternate ending.
Example: it can be gradovima or gradoviman

Rule: For feminine nouns, it's shortened. 
ženama=>ženan

Rule:-ao endings are shortened to just -a
Example:
išao je => iša je
rekao sam => reka san

Rule:-io endings change to -ija
Example:
govorio je => govorija je
vidio sam => vidija san

Rule:ča, aside from meaning "what" can also be used as a particle meaning "away" or "out".
Example:
gremo ča (let's get out of here)

Rule: some of the third person plural forms can often be extended into longer -u ending forms.
Example:
govore -> govoru -> govoridu
rade -> radu -> radidu
pišu -> pišedu

Rule: Infinitive is shortened .
There is no I at the end.
In some speeches there is neither T/Ć at the end
Example: 
bit =>bi
pivat =>piva
znat => zna
plivat => pliva
ronit => roni

Rule: change lj=>j
Example:
zaljubiti se=>zajubit se
ljubav =>jubav
ljuska =>juska
ljudi=>judi

Rule: Change O => E in some cases
Example:
nekoga-nikega
svakoga-svakega
tomu-temu
toga-tega
bijeloga-bilega
jednoga-jenega
jednomu-jenemu

Rule: In standard croatian third person plural has ending in some verbs E. In chakavian it is always U
Examples:
vide =>vidu
hoće =>hoću
stoje =>stoju
stave =>stavu
motre =>motru
leže=>ležu

Rule: Change H=>V
Examples:
kruh =>kruv
kuhati =>kuvati
suh =>suv
gluh =>gluv[/b]
